Lets be honest. Its hard to argue with the fact that no one knows a campus quite as well as the students who trek from end to end each and every day. Backhand allows students to help eachother out by giving them to power to generate content.

So what gives?

The Problem

College can be a lot of things, the most likely of which is a little overwhelming. There's places to be and people to meet, but it can be hard to know when or where to go. After almost 5 years on campus, I still found myself discovering new places until the day before I graduated. I constantly found myself asking questions like "Where can I print this report? Are any of those cool phone charging stations around here?" Perhaps most importantly - "Where can I meet some like-minded friends?"

The Solution

Introducing Backhand. This mobile application solves the problem by giving students a community driven framework to share their favorite places on campus. Users can visit these spots and either up-vote, down-vote, or leave a comment with their thoughts on the location, with the best spots slowly emerging over time. Certain established entities on campus could also have the power to "sticky" their location, allowing any club or organization to quite literally "get themselves on the map". But lets be honest - College is all about the people you meet and the relationships you form. In addition to adding static points of interest... events, or "shouts", can also be added by students who are looking . Users can then see these shouts in real time on their map, depending on the map layers they have enabled, and RSVP to those that interest them. This can be anything from a 3 on 3 pick-up game of basketball or a tutoring session going on in the library.
